About Us...


The ALGAE protocol was produced through a joint collaboration between Imperial College's Small Area Health Statistics Unit and the University of Bristol's ALSPAC cohort. It was developed as a generic automated solution to support work in an environmental health study that is examining the relationship between early life exposure to air pollution and later life respiratory health in cohort members.

The project has been as much a collaboration between the data management teams of SAHSU and ALSPAC as it has been a collaboration between its research teams. Many people from both teams have been involved with the project and their contributions are described in detail below.

The Small Area Health Statistics Unit Team

Dr. Kevin Garwood Data Scientist
  • designed and developed a completely automated solution to support the initial environmental health study use case.
  • developed the method of assessing exposure measurement error that took into account the nature of the administrative data used to provide residential address histories.
  • developed the means auditing changes and quantifying the effects through sensitivity variables.
  • developed the means of calculating exposure assessments through multiple methods and comparing their results.
  • wrote most of the web site.
Dr. Daniela Fecht Exposure Scientist
  • The main source of expertise in all aspects of geocoding and troubleshooting potential errors in the activity.
  • Explained the protocol from the perspective of an exposure scientist for the web site and reviewed other aspects of the web site.
  • Identified criteria for identifying invalid geocode and out-of-bounds geocodes.
  • identified need to capture covariate data for each move to help assess the relationship between exposure results and social mobility over time.
  • Along with John Gulliver, described use case scenarios where exposure areas may exhibit high levels of spatial and temporal variation in concentrations of pollutants.
Dr. John Gulliver Exposure Scientist
  • Generated exposure data for the use case, which defined the attributes that are needed in ALGAE's original_exposure_data table.
  • Helped define the behaviour of the uncleaned mobility assessment
  • Decided that the best measure of difference between exposure values generated through different methods was to use a percent error calculation.
  • Along with Daniela Fecht, described use case scenarios where exposure areas may exhibit high levels of spatial and temporal variation in concentrations of pollutants.
Dr. Anna Hansell Epidemiologist

Ms Clare Pearson Epidemiologist
  • helped draft original analysis protocol
  • was involved with original data analysis to help understand the effect of gaps and overlaps in the administrative records used to produce residential address histories.
  • identified cohort questionnaire variables that led to the use of the absent_during_exp_period and at_1st_addr_conception fields in the original_study_member_data.

The ALSPAC Team

Mr Iain Bickerstaffe Data Manager
  • Described the process for how address period records were originally created through the software system that ALSPAC used to track the current addresses of its cohort members. This was crucially important in understanding the difference between the original intent of collecting the records and the intent of using them to support a high resolution exposure assessment.
  • Provided reasons why exposure days might be missing from the exposure data that were used in the use case study. His feedback led to adding a sensitivity variable to describe missing exposure days.
Dr. Olly Butters Data Manager
  • Along with Peter Hambly, he provided great insight into the future trends of information governance that would govern how the use case study would need to be done as an activity between a cohort centre and a partnering research institution.
  • allocated secure computing resources for programming work to be done by Imperial staff at ALSPAC's campus in Bristol.
Tim Morris Data Administrator
  • Along with Iain Bickerstaffe, Tim recalled the administrative processes that had been used to track changes of addresses in cohort members.
  • Provided insight into anomalies in some of the address period records.
Dr. Kate Northstone Epidemiologist
  • Provided Imperial College researchers with ALSPAC data related to health outcomes and life stages. Her data set helped influence what generic fields would appear in the original_study_member_data table.
  • Provided guidance about information governance in the use case study.
